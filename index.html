Guide to Foreign Contract Income & Tax Compliance

For Malaysian remote workers & freelancers earning in foreign currencies

⚖️

Is Foreign Income Taxable in Malaysia?

As a remote software engineer, data architect, or designer working from Malaysia for a foreign entity (US, Singapore, Europe), your income is classified as Paragraph 4(a) Gains or Profits from a Business or independent contract work. Because the work is physically executed within Malaysia, it is considered derived locally and is fully subject to LHDN progressive income tax rates.

Utilizing a monthly Potongan Cukai Berjadual (PCB) simulator helps remote professionals manually set aside tax reserves to avoid heavy penalties or overwhelming lump-sum payments when filing the annual Form B or Form BE.

🏦

Optimizing Voluntary EPF & SOCSO Compliance

Foreign companies are not legally obligated to remit statutory contributions to the Employees Provident Fund (EPF) or PERKESO (SOCSO/EIS). However, Malaysian freelancers can opt into the i-Saraan program or voluntary self-contributions.

Voluntary EPF contributions up to 11% (or custom thresholds) not only safeguard retirement yields but also unlock specific tax relief assessment quotas up to RM 4,000 annually, lowering your true net chargeable income.
